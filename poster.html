Nuclear Energy Derating Forecaster

A 14-day, weather-driven capacity-factor forecast for U.S. nuclear units, with calibrated uncertainty bands and an LLM-generated operator briefing.

HackArizona · Team 10

The Problem

During heatwaves and low-flow conditions, once-through-cooled reactors are forced to derate — running below 100% nameplate to stay within thermal-discharge limits. Grid operators get little advance notice; current public reporting is same-day or after-the-fact.

Our Approach

One XGBoost model per forecast horizon (1–14 days), trained only on day-of weather and water features. A symmetric residual band gives calibrated uncertainty; AWS Bedrock + Gemma 3 27B turns the numeric output into a plain-English briefing for non-experts.

Datasets

  • NRC Daily Power Status — capacity-factor labels, 2005–present
  • Open-Meteo ERA5 archive + historical-forecast + live (8 hourly vars: temp, dew point, humidity, wind, shortwave, precip, surface pressure, cloud cover)
  • USGS NWIS daily values — water temp (00010) & discharge (00060)
  • EIA-860 annual release — plant catalog, nameplate, lat/lon

~7,300 training rows per plant after outage / pre-outage filtering. Test split: 1,026 days, ~22 dip events per horizon.

Model Architecture

Algorithm
14× XGBoost regressors (one per horizon)
Hyperparams
depth 6 · lr 0.05 · 1500 rounds · ES 50 · subsample 0.8
Loss
squared-error w/ dip-weighted samples (α = 0.5)
Calibration
Isotonic, gated on raw ≥ 95% (preserves dip recall)
Band
Symmetric residual: point ± δh, δ from 80th-pct |val residuals|
Attribution
Tree-SHAP via pred_contribs=True — exact, per-horizon
Splits
train ≤ 2019 · val 2020–2022 · test 2023+

Feature Engineering

  • Lags: 1, 3, 7, 14 d (matches inference horizons)
  • Rolling: 7, 14, 30 d means & maxes (closed='left' — no same-day leakage)
  • Heat dose: Σ max(0, Tmax − 25 °C) over 7 / 14 d — proxy for thermal-limit pressure
  • Categorical: USGS site id (handles multi-gauge plants natively)
  • Outage / pre-outage rows filtered from training; surfaced separately at inference.

All artifacts (forecast, attributions, backtest, briefing) are precomputed daily on Railway and uploaded to a single forecast_artifacts Postgres table. The API ships no data — it streams cached bytes with a 5-min TTL and a 60s refresh watcher.
